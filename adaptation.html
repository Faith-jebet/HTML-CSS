Adaptation to the impacts of climate change

Adaptation Strategies for Farmers

Farmers can take the following steps:

  • Plant drought-resistant crops.

    • Example of drought resistant crops:

    1. Quinoa
    2. Millet
    3. Sorghum

  • Improve soil quality through sustainable practices.

  • Implement water-saving technologies.

    • Get more information on the conditions of various crops:

    1. Maize: Maize thrives in warm temperatures between 60-95°F (15-35°C)
      with well-distributed rainfall or irrigation during the growing season.
      It requires a frost-free growing season of about 90-120 days.
    2. Potatoes: Potatoes prefer cool climates with temperatures between 60-70°F (15-21°C) during the
      growing season.They require well-drained, loose soil with consistent moisture.
    3. Sorghum: Sorghum is a warm-season crop that prefers temperatures between 75-85°F (24-29°C) during the growing season.
      It is relatively drought-tolerant and can grow in areas with limited rainfall.
    4. Wheat: Wheat grows best in cool, temperate climates with temperatures between 50-70°F (10-21°C).
      It requires well-drained soil and adequate moisture during its growth stages.
    5. Potatoes: Potatoes prefer cool climates with temperatures between 60-70°F (15-21°C) during the growing season.
      They require well-drained, loose soil with consistent moisture.
    6. Beans: Beans prefer warm temperatures ranging from 70-80°F (21-27°C) during the day and slightly cooler nights.
      They require well-drained soil and regular, even moisture.
